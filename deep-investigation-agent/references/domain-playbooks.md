# Domain Playbooks
Use these domain-specific rules as overlays on top of the core workflow.
## Geopolitics and public policy
Prioritize:
- official statements,
- legal texts,
- multilateral documents,
- local reporting,
- wire services,
- budget documents,
- sanctions lists,
- procurement,
- shipping or customs data,
- satellite or geospatial evidence where relevant.
Watch for:
- strategic ambiguity,
- signaling vs action,
- domestic political incentives,
- coalition constraints,
- staged leaks,
- retrospective narrative framing.
## Macroeconomics, trade, and finance
Prioritize:
- central bank releases,
- finance ministry data,
- national statistics,
- IMF / World Bank / OECD / WTO material,
- company filings,
- trade records,
- bond, FX, commodity, and earnings context.
Watch for:
- nominal vs real confusion,
- annualized vs absolute misreading,
- base effects,
- one-off distortions,
- liquidity vs solvency confusion,
- short-term market moves mistaken for structural change.
## Corporate and ownership investigations
Prioritize:
- registries,
- annual reports,
- investor presentations,
- beneficial ownership clues,
- litigation,
- debt disclosures,
- merger filings,
- procurement records,
- board overlap,
- legal representation overlap,
- repeated shell entities,
- customs or shipping traces.
Watch for:
- nominee structures,
- affiliate relationships,
- hidden dependency through lenders or distributors,
- governance/control divergence from public branding.
## Technology and cyber
Prioritize:
- official docs,
- changelogs,
- CVEs,
- incident advisories,
- commit history,
- repositories,
- infrastructure metadata,
- standards references,
- vendor disclosures,
- direct reproduction or proof-of-concept evidence where appropriate.
Watch for:
- marketing claims outpacing implementation,
- ambiguous severity language,
- recycled technical writeups,
- exploit attribution without sufficient basis.
## Medicine, epidemics, and public health
Prioritize:
- guidelines,
- surveillance reports,
- registries,
- systematic reviews,
- strong primary papers,
- transparent methods,
- effect sizes,
- uncertainty intervals,
- baseline risk.
Watch for:
- anecdote inflation,
- relative-risk framing without absolute risk,
- preprint overconfidence,
- subgroup overinterpretation,
- causal claims from observational data without caution.
## Humanitarian, food security, and crisis response
Prioritize:
- UN and humanitarian assessments,
- health-system capacity data,
- displacement data,
- food prices,
- logistics and access constraints,
- conflict maps,
- infrastructure damage and restoration context.
Watch for:
- aggregate claims that hide local variation,
- outdated situation snapshots,
- headline casualty framing without source lineage,
- policy claims detached from operational capacity.
## Narrative, media, and influence analysis
Prioritize:
- source lineage,
- earliest appearance,
- framing differences,
- outlet incentives,
- synchronized language,
- distribution pathways,
- beneficiary analysis.
Watch for:
- laundering of weak claims through repetition,
- selective clipping,
- coordinated narrative bursts,
- false balance,
- moral framing used as an evidence substitute.
