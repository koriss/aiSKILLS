SECTION 1: CLASSIC ACADEMIC TEXTS
================================================================================

TITLE: Thought Reform and the Psychology of Totalism: A Study of 'Brainwashing' in China
AUTHOR: Robert Jay Lifton
YEAR: 1961 (reissued 1989 with new preface)
DESCRIPTION: The foundational text on thought reform. Lifton identifies 8 criteria of psychological manipulation: milieu control, mystical manipulation, demand for purity, confession culture, loading the language, thought stopping, doctrine over person, and dispensing of existence. Based on interviews with former Chinese prisoners.
URL: https://archive.org/details/thoughtreformpsy0000lift
FILE_PATH: propaganda-library/lifton_thought_reform_1961.pdf

TITLE: Coercive Persuasion: A Socio-Psychological Analysis of the "Brainwashing" of American Civilian Prisoners by the Chinese Communists
AUTHOR: Edgar H. Schein (with Inge Schneier and Curtis H. Barker)
YEAR: 1961
DESCRIPTION: Early academic analysis of coercive persuasion techniques used on Korean War POWs. Schein's work predates Lifton and focuses on the socio-psychological mechanisms of conversion.
URL: https://archive.org/details/coercivepersuasi0000sche
FILE_PATH: propaganda-library/schein_coercive_persuasion_1961.pdf

TITLE: Obedience to Authority
AUTHOR: Stanley Milgram
YEAR: 1974
DESCRIPTION: Milgram's book documenting his famous obedience experiments. Shows how ordinary people follow authority even to the point of harming others. Essential for understanding compliance mechanisms.
URL: https://archive.org/details/ObedienceToAuthority_368
FILE_PATH: propaganda-library/milgram_obedience_1974.pdf

SECTION 2: CONTEMPORARY CULT STUDIES
================================================================================

TITLE: Combatting Cult Mind Control
AUTHOR: Steven Hassan
YEAR: 1988 (updated edition 2018)
DESCRIPTION: The BITE Model (Behavior, Information, Thought, Emotion) was developed by Hassan to describe how cults recruit and maintain control. The 2018 updated edition covers internet, trafficking, and terrorism.
URL: https://archive.org/details/combattingcultmi0000hass
FILE_PATH: propaganda-library/hassan_combating_cult_mind_control_1988.pdf

TITLE: Cults in Our Midst: The Continuing Fight Against Their Hidden Menace
AUTHOR: Margaret Thaler Singer (with Janja Lalich)
YEAR: 1996 (revised 2003)
DESCRIPTION: Comprehensive study of cult recruitment tactics, persuasion techniques, and organizational manipulation. Singer was a key figure in the APA's task force on cults.
URL: https://archive.org/details/cultsinourmidst0000sing
FILE_PATH: propaganda-library/singer_cults_in_our_midst_1996.pdf

TITLE: The Lucifer Effect: Understanding How Good People Turn Evil
AUTHOR: Philip Zimbardo
YEAR: 2007
DESCRIPTION: Zimbardo's book about the Stanford Prison Experiment, exploring the situational factors that lead to abusive behavior. Includes analysis of Abu Ghraib.
URL: http://pdf.prisonexp.org/ijcp1973.pdf
FILE_PATH: propaganda-library/zimbardo_lucifer_effect_2007.pdf
